# RFC 0001 — Shuttle v0.2: declarative, RDF-native, self-describing, lattice-modular

*Status: DESIGN / for review (not yet implemented). Synthesized by a 3-paradigm design panel. This supersedes the surface of v0.1 (spec/SHUTTLE.md); the relational core and the streaming/serializer machinery survive.*


## Recommended approach

HYBRID, with **pure-attribute-grammar-rdf as the spine**, hardened by two specific imports from relational-rdf-native and four from construct-templates. Verdict per proposal: (1) pure-attribute-grammar-rdf wins on theory pedigree and writability — classical Knuth L-AG with chained attributes and copy-rule convention is the clearest declarative story, the most checkable (50 years of AG dependency analysis), and by far the most writable (`shu:chain env` + absent-equation-means-copy means authors write only what differs, exactly where v0.1 wrote `:=`); its profile rules (profiles remove only sugar, never guard-free fallbacks; profiles import only profiles) give L3 print totality *by construction*; its negative-test accretion (mechanical LL-table membership re-check, re-tag `shu:negativeFor`) is the most precise of the three. (2) From relational-rdf-native we take the **exported strictness contracts** (`shu:strictness` Immediate | AfterTokens(k) | AtEnd on every production — the one mechanism that makes streaming schedules *contractually* stable under extension: a caller's schedule depends only on callees' strictness classes, never their clause lists, so adding the tripleTerm alternative to `nt:object` provably cannot reschedule collection emission) and the **Hereditary/ThisLanguage negative-test scoping** with compiler-verified hereditary negatives. We REJECT its `shu:refines` override: its own limitations section concedes refinement conservativity is only differentially tested, which grows the trusted kernel; the one case that motivates it (Turtle's `subject predicateObjectList '.'` vs N-Triples' `subject predicate object '.'` — same FIRST set, so naive alternative-addition is an LL conflict) is solved instead by **pre-factoring the lattice bottom**: `ntriples11` defines `nt:statement ::= subject predicateObjectList '.'` where N-Triples' predicateObjectList has exactly one degenerate alternative (`verb object`, no separators) — same language, W3C-Turtle-shaped spine, and Turtle then extends purely monotonically by adding `;`/`,` chain alternatives. Extension stays 100% monotone; no override mechanism exists in v0.2 core. (3) From construct-templates we take the **hash-pinned import closure (lockfile)** that closes RDF's open world into a sound compile unit, the **interface summary graphs** for incremental compilation, **`shu:resultLift IntoDefaultGraph`** for triples→quads test lifting plus the tiny `graph-labels` module with **module-closed constant folding** (the Turtle parser carries no graph slot even though every template mentions `ctx.g`), the **trig-w3c profile** answer to the N-Quads⊂TriG W3C-fidelity wrinkle, and the **v0.1↔v0.2 IR α-equivalence differential gate** in CI. Namespace: `shu:` = https://w3id.org/shuttle/vocab#, `shux:` = https://w3id.org/shuttle/expr#, modules under https://w3id.org/shuttle/mod/.


## Rationale

Criterion-by-criterion: (1) DECLARATIVITY — all three achieve zero imperative residue via the same four moves (threads→structural recursion, `env.x :=`→chained-attribute equations, label table→Skolem functions of (scope,label), ordered emit blocks→unordered template sets); AG's is the most *conventional* purity: the chain/copy-rule convention is standard Kastens/UUAG machinery, so every claim ("prefix maps defined by earlier statements is just L-attributed inherited flow") is a textbook theorem, not a novel argument a reviewer must trust. CT's Δ-monoid is elegant but an extra algebraic gadget for the same effect; REL's Horn-clause least-fixpoint is equivalent but pushes reviewers toward logic-programming intuitions the compiler never actually uses. (2) RDF-NATIVENESS + SELF-DESCRIPTION — tie on substance (all three: grammars are named graphs, vocabulary constants appear as themselves, ontology + SHACL shapes, meta-syntax IS Turtle, .shu surface demoted to an ordinary module emitting shu: triples). AG adds the distinctive `shu:MonotoneProperty`/`shu:FunctionalGrammarProperty` self-classification — the vocabulary describes which of its own predicates are open vs closed, which is exactly the reflective closure the criterion asks for. REL's Fresh-as-blank-nodes-in-the-grammar-graph is maximally native but its own limitations call it "a real foot-gun" (template existentials vs structural rdf:List bnodes); we use an explicit `shux:Skolem` expression node instead — one step less cute, categorically unambiguous. (3) LATTICE SHARING BEYOND GRAMMAR — the deciding criterion. AG's "the lattice IS the L3 fallback chain" (imported N-Triples alternatives are the guard-free print fallbacks of every superset) is the single best idea in the pool: serializer sharing is free, and profile soundness (remove only sugar) preserves it by construction. AG also has the only fully-mechanical negative-test story (decidable superset-membership re-check per accreted negative). CT contributes the missing quad-lift plumbing (`resultLift`) and the honest trig-w3c profile. REL's `conformsTo`+import-closure suite definition is adopted as the suite semantics. Rejecting `shu:refines` in favor of pre-factoring keeps the monotonicity theorem (M ≤ M' ⟹ L(M) ⊆ L(M') and agreement on L(M)) unconditional — no conservativity obligations, no differential proof search in the kernel; the residual risk (pre-factored N-Triples fidelity) is discharged by the CT coherence check profile(turtle11, fallbacks-only) ≅ ntriples11 plus a language-equivalence test against the W3C N-Triples EBNF. (4) STREAMING — all three derive the schedule (L-attribution check, template groundness points, chain/accumulator linearization, Skolem→counter fusion); only REL makes it *stable under extension by contract* via exported strictness classes, so that goes in. AG's prediction-time-synthesis analysis (cellChain.head ground at clause selection) is the sharpest account of why structural recursion streams; both are kept — strictness is the interface, prediction-time groundness the inference that discharges it. (5) PERFORMANCE — identical architecture in all three: RDF graph is compile-time only, front-end swap above the unchanged v0.1 shared IR (explicit-stack resumable machine, suspendable token DFA, reverse match plans), existing gen-js backend, so the ~2x-over-N3.js result transfers by construction; CT's differential gate (v0.1 turtle12.shuttle and the v0.2 module graph must lower to α-equivalent IR) turns "by construction" into a CI-checked fact, and REL's module-erasure guarantee (composed artifact bit-identical whether authored monolithically or as ∪ of deltas) makes "modularity has zero runtime cost" testable. Q8 benchmark gate re-runs per composed artifact; note the live N3.js perf PR series (#635, #642–#649) moves the baseline, so the claim is re-earned against a pinned N3.js version. (6) RDF 1.2 STATIC — all three identical and adopted: typed template positions (shu:s ⊑ SubjT, shu:p ⊑ Iri) validated by SHACL over the merged closure; `shux:tt : (SubjT, Iri, Term) → TripleTermT ⊑ Term ⋢ SubjT` gives nests-via-object-only for free; `"x"@en^^dt` stays unlexable by clause shape; deltas only add constructors so 1.1 modules compile unchanged against 1.2 closures. (7) CLARITY/WRITABILITY — AG decisively: chain declaration, copy rules, `fold` sugar on repetitions (macro-defined, no independent semantics), anchored regex literals with optional structured form (vs CT's verbose structural-regex-with-shortcut-wart). The .shu surface stays the authoring format with the RDF graph as canonical projection, and the surface-module rule ("any surface feature must have an RDF projection by construction") prevents drift.


## The declarative model (every imperative v0.1 construct, removed)

SEMANTIC MODEL — a Shuttle v0.2 module denotes, for each IRI-named nonterminal N, a typed attribute signature inh(N)/syn(N) and a set of alternatives; each alternative carries (i) an ordered syntax row (the ONE ordered thing, because concatenation is ordered — a closed rdf:List), (ii) an UNORDERED set of pure equations, one per defined attribute occurrence, statically checked acyclic and single-assignment, (iii) an UNORDERED set of triple templates ⟨s,p,o[,g][,when]⟩ with SPARQL-CONSTRUCT semantics, (iv) an unordered set of constraints (require→diagnostic resources with stable error codes; emptiness of the auto-unioned diags attribute defines validity), (v) Skolem binders. The meaning of a document is the unique solution of the equation system on its LL-deterministic parse tree; the emitted graph is the set union of template instances over the tree (multiset union under an explicit `shu:emits …Bag` module for line-oriented profiles). Parse/print/generate remain three groundness modes of this one relation; print is the unchanged v0.1 residual-consumption reading (one covering instantiation per template occurrence; Skolem sites are linear bnode matches). EVERY v0.1 IMPERATIVE CONSTRUCT, GONE: (1) `env.x := e` → a `shu:chain env : Env` declaration per module; the statement spine threads it via generated Knuth copy rules; a directive is one equation `envOut = env{prefixes ↦ bind(env.prefixes, ns, resolve(env.base, i))}`; a triples statement contributes the identity. (2) `thread v : T = init in (…)*` → structural recursion over the right-recursive chain nonterminal v0.1 already admitted it desugars to; collections are the natural unfold (cellChain cons: ν cell; head = cell; templates {cell rdf:first o, cell rdf:rest tail.head}); the pending-reifier `pend` is an inherited SubjT? parameter of annotationChain — scope safety is lexical (the attribute exists only on the chain), not a reset discipline. Authors may write `(…)* fold(v : T from init, step = e)` sugar, defined by macro-expansion to exactly this chain. (3) blank-node label table → deleted: `_:x` denotes the pure term `skolem(scope, "x")` over an inherited `scope` attribute (constant doc for Turtle; the formula's own Skolem for N3 — push/pop becomes one parameter equation); `[]`/fresh sites are `shux:Skolem` nodes denoting bnodeAt(derivation-path, k). Deterministic `_:b0, _:b1…` conformance labels are recovered because document-order enumeration of Skolem positions is a bijection — the compiler fuses the Skolem function back into v0.1's counter, now a *correct implementation* of a pure semantics. (4) ordered `{c1; c2}` blocks and the seven verbs → four order-free categories: equations (value/let/envOut), templates (emit+when), constraints (require), Skolems (fresh); `oracle` is a declared pure decidable predicate; `e ?? d` is a total equation over option types. STREAMING RECONCILIATION — order-freedom licenses streaming: since the denotation is a set, any emission order is correct, so the compiler picks the earliest, via four derived analyses replacing v0.1's authored discipline: (a) L-attributedness check per alternative over the equation dependency graph (classic ordered-AG test; failure yields the offending cycle; `shu:buffered true` is the visible opt-out); (b) groundness-point analysis per template, with prediction-time synthesis (attributes computable from clause selection + constants + Skolems + inherited attrs are ground at the callee's first token — this is why `cell rdf:rest tail.head` fires as each element closes, and why ν pre-availability lets v0.2 emit `:s :p _:c0` at '(' — one token EARLIER than v0.1's hand-placed emit); (c) linearization: tail-recursive chains compile to loops (O(depth + |env| + token) memory), single-threaded chained attributes compile to one in-place record (the compiler *introduces* the mutation v0.1 made authors write; genuinely duplicated envs — N3 formulae — fall back per-field to an O(1)-amortized undo log); (d) Skolem→counter fusion. Every production EXPORTS a `shu:strictness` class (Immediate | AfterTokens(k) | AtEnd) that deltas must keep — schedule stability under extension is contractual (see lattice field). PERF — front-end swap only: grammar graph → SHACL → AG analyses → the UNCHANGED v0.1 shared IR → existing backends; RDF processing is compile-time only; module structure is erased before codegen (bit-identical artifact regardless of module topology); module-closed constant folding drops the graph slot from the Turtle parser; CI carries the v0.1↔v0.2 IR α-equivalence gate and the pinned Q8 harness. RDF 1.2 — rdf12-terms adds `shux:tt : (SubjT, Iri, Term) → TripleTermT` with TripleTermT ⊑ Term, ⋢ SubjT (object-only nesting is a subtype fact), `dir`, rdf:dirLangString, and LANG_DIR `shu:extendsTerminal` LANGTAG; the tripleTerm alternative is PURE (no template — inner triple unasserted); version directive is one env equation; all checked statically by SHACL positional typing over the merged closure before the compiler runs.


## The `shtl:` RDF grammar vocabulary

Namespaces: `shu:` https://w3id.org/shuttle/vocab# (ontology in shu.ttl: RDFS/OWL classes + SHACL shapes, itself an importable module), `shux:` https://w3id.org/shuttle/expr#, modules under https://w3id.org/shuttle/mod/. A module is a NAMED GRAPH whose name is the module IRI; extensible resources (productions, alternatives, templates, terminals, tests, policies, isos) MUST be IRI-identified; bnodes are legal only for non-extensible internals (items, exprs) — SHACL-enforced. MODULE LAYER: shu:Module; shu:imports (⊑ owl:imports; acyclic DAG; semantics = named-graph union of the transitive closure, compiled only against a hash-pinned lockfile graphIRI→content-hash); shu:Profile ⊑ shu:Module with shu:profileOf, shu:excludes, shu:includesOnly; shu:start; shu:specRef (mandatory W3C pin); shu:emits (shu:TripleSet|QuadSet|TripleBag|QuadBag); shu:chain (declares an inh/syn Env pair with generated copy-rule threading). SYNTAX LAYER: shu:Production (shu:inh/shu:syn → shu:AttrDecl [shu:name, shu:type, shu:default]; shu:strictness → shu:Immediate | [a shu:AfterTokens; shu:k n] | shu:AtEnd — the exported streaming contract; shu:buffered); shu:Alternative with shu:alternativeOf (THE extension point: an OPEN predicate — a production's alternative set is whatever the import closure asserts; sound because v0.1 proved alternatives are order-independent/LL-disjoint); shu:items (closed rdf:List — order lives here only); item classes shu:Lit(shu:text), shu:TerminalRef, shu:NonterminalRef(shu:calls, shu:args, shu:bind), shu:Opt/shu:Star/shu:Plus/shu:SepList(shu:of, shu:sep) each with a NORMATIVE desugaring into fresh right-recursive productions; `fold` sugar likewise. SEMANTIC LAYER: shu:Equation (shu:onAlternative, shu:target [occurrence = shu:ofItem + shu:attr, or own syn attr], shu:expr, shu:otherwise); ABSENT equation = Knuth copy rule (this convention is what makes attribute-row extension free); shu:TripleTemplate (shu:onAlternative — open — shu:s, shu:p, shu:o, shu:g [default: inherited g], shu:when); shu:Constraint (shu:onAlternative, shu:test, shu:errorCode — the negative-test generator hook); expressions: shux:App(shux:fn, shux:args), shux:AttrRef(shux:item, shux:attr), shux:VarRef, shux:Case, shux:Skolem (explicit node — NOT bare bnodes, avoiding the Fresh/structural-bnode ambiguity), constants as the RDF terms THEMSELVES (rdf:first in a template is the IRI rdf:first). Types from mod:rdf-terms: shu:Term, shu:SubjT, shu:Iri, shu:GraphT, shu:TripleTermT, shu:Env, scalars; constructor signatures as triples (shux:tt shu:sig (shu:SubjT shu:Iri shu:Term); shu:returns shu:TripleTermT). Any production may synthesize any term-algebra subtype AND carry templates (resolving NOTE 2 — "T!" is no longer a special semType, just syn typing + template presence). TERMINAL LAYER: shu:Terminal (shu:pattern — anchored regex literal "…"^^shu:regex plus optional structured form for auditing; functional, one per closure); shu:fragment; shu:skip true (resolves NOTE 1 — skip status is data, unioned across the closure, FunctionalGrammarProperty per terminal); shu:valueIso/shu:unparseIso → shu:Iso resources (shu:forwardFn, shu:backwardFn, shu:lossy + shu:canonicalizer — the audited v0.1 §5 iso library, IRI-identified in mod:rdf-terms); shu:extendsTerminal (declared language-superset obligation, e.g. LANG_DIR ⊒ LANGTAG, checked by lexical conservativity); shu:reservedAgainst (tie-breaks as queryable triples). PRINT LAYER: shu:prefer (xsd:decimal on alternatives); shu:printGuard → guard expressions over a CLOSED, IRI-identified guard vocabulary in mod:print-core (shu:listShaped, shu:freshSingleUse, shu:reifiesQuadPresent, shu:needsLongQuoting, shu:abbreviable — resolving NOTE 4; each guard resource declares shu:requiresIndex naming the residual/window index that decides it, feeding derived-index computation; extensions may add guard IRIs with declared index needs — monotone); shu:PrintPolicy (shu:orderBy, shu:groupBySubject, shu:prefixMining, shu:directivePlacement, shu:layout — the Q4 vocabulary as properties); guard-free fallback alternatives live in the LEAST module declaring the production. TEST LAYER: shu:TestSuite (shu:suiteFor; suites are modules; Suite(M) = tests of the import closure); shu:EvalTest/shu:PositiveSyntaxTest/shu:NegativeSyntaxTest ⊑ the W3C rdft: classes with mf:action/mf:result reused verbatim (W3C manifests import directly); shu:negativeScope (shu:ThisLanguage default | shu:Hereditary — compiler-VERIFIED against every composed superset); shu:negativeFor (re-tagging after superset membership re-check); shu:resultLift (e.g. [shu:onSuite …; shu:lift shu:IntoDefaultGraph] for triples→quads edges). REFLECTIVE LAYER: shu:MonotoneProperty (extension may add: alternativeOf, onAlternative, inh-fields-with-defaults, suiteFor, prefer-ranked candidates, guards, tests, terminals, constructors) vs shu:FunctionalGrammarProperty (at most one in the closure: pattern per terminal, equation per occurrence, synthesizes-type per production, strictness per production, guard-free fallback per group) — the vocabulary classifies its own predicates; violations are hard errors reported WITH BOTH NAMED GRAPHS (why modules must be named graphs). TURTLE EXAMPLE (collection cons cell): ```turtle ttl:cellChain a shu:Production ; shu:syn [shu:name "head"; shu:type shu:Term] ; shu:strictness [a shu:AfterTokens; shu:k 1] . ttl:cellChain-cons a shu:Alternative ; shu:alternativeOf ttl:cellChain ; shu:items ( [a shu:NonterminalRef; shu:calls nt:object; shu:bind "o"] [a shu:NonterminalRef; shu:calls ttl:cellChain; shu:bind "t"] ) ; shu:let [shu:name "cell"; shu:expr [a shux:Skolem]] ; shu:eq [shu:target "head"; shu:expr [a shux:VarRef; shux:name "cell"]] . ttl:cc-first a shu:TripleTemplate ; shu:onAlternative ttl:cellChain-cons ; shu:s [a shux:VarRef; shux:name "cell"] ; shu:p rdf:first ; shu:o [a shux:AttrRef; shux:item "o"; shux:attr "value"] ; shu:g [a shux:AttrRef; shux:attr "g"] . ttl:cc-rest a shu:TripleTemplate ; shu:onAlternative ttl:cellChain-cons ; shu:s [a shux:VarRef; shux:name "cell"] ; shu:p rdf:rest ; shu:o [a shux:AttrRef; shux:item "t"; shux:attr "head"] ; shu:g [a shux:AttrRef; shux:attr "g"] .``` Note the deliberate ouroboros: shu:items uses the very rdf:first/rdf:rest cells the collection production defines.


## Lattice import / extension (sharing beyond grammar)

MECHANISM — module = named graph; shu:imports = transitive named-graph union (idempotent, so the TriG diamond over N-Triples is free); compilation always against a hash-pinned lockfile closing the open world; each compiled module exports an INTERFACE SUMMARY GRAPH (per-production FIRST/FOLLOW contributions, strictness classes, attribute-dependency summaries, groundness frontiers, token automaton, print-guard index needs, context-row type, content hash) so composition is summary-based and incremental. EXTENSION is monotone triple-addition ONLY: new alternatives on visible production IRIs, templates on your OWN alternatives, terminals, constructors, chain-field widenings with defaults (imported equations never mention new fields, so they lift via copy rules unchanged; env compiles to a single record pointer, so imported COMPILED code is ABI-stable — N-Triples' compiled productions serve Turtle unmodified), prefer-ranked guarded print candidates, tests. No override of any kind: displacing an imported equation/template/pattern is a FunctionalGrammarProperty conflict reported with both graph names. Where the subset relation forces a shape change (the statement seam), the lattice bottom is PRE-FACTORED instead: nt:statement ::= subject predicateObjectList '.' with N-Triples' pOL having one degenerate alternative (verb object); Turtle adds the ';'/',' chain alternatives monotonically. Four delta-checked contracts make extension safe: (i) LL(k≤2) disjointness of new alternatives against imported FIRST summaries (fail = counterexample naming both alternative IRIs and home graphs; never silent reshaping — imported parses are bitwise identical); (ii) lexical conservativity: merged token DFA must tokenize the imported token language identically (LANG_DIR passes); (iii) strictness contracts: a new alternative binding the synthesized value later than the production's declared class is rejected AT THE DELTA — callers' schedules depend only on strictness classes, so no import can demote a streaming production to buffered; (iv) L3 stability: guard-free fallbacks pinned to the least declaring module survive any extension. RESTRICTION only via shu:Profile: shu:excludes/includesOnly, may only remove SUGAR (never a guard-free fallback ⇒ L(profile) ⊆ L(base) and print totality automatic), no dangling references, profiles import only profiles (an extension cannot un-exclude). Prefer-ties across diamonds: conflict, resolved explicitly, never silently — ties affect prettiness only. THE LATTICE — mod:rdf-terms (term algebra, isos, guard vocabulary, SHACL shapes; defined ONCE) ← mod:ntriples11 (terminals IRIREF/STRING_LITERAL_QUOTE/BLANK_NODE_LABEL/LANGTAG + WS/COMMENT skips; the pre-factored spine; ONE template ⟨s,p,o,@g⟩ where inherited g : GraphT defaults to shu:defaultGraph — declared at the bottom, exploited two rungs up; canonical one-line print policy; ntriples11-tests). TURTLE11 = imports ntriples11 + delta: PNAME/ANON/number/boolean/string terminals; shu:chain env gains prefixes+base (row widening, zero edits to imported productions); directive productions (pure env equations); pOL/objectList chain alternatives, collections, bnpl — each ADDED to imported choice points (ttl:iri-pname shu:alternativeOf nt:iri; ttl:object-coll shu:alternativeOf nt:object); prefer/guards on sugar alternatives — the imported N-Triples alternatives ARE the guard-free fallbacks: THE LATTICE IS THE L3 CHAIN, so the serializer is inherited, not rewritten, and "canonical N-Triples output of any Turtle graph" is just printing under the ntriples profile. NQUADS11 = imports ntriples11 + graph-labels (the g column concept): one production delta (optional graph label binding g); ZERO semantic delta — the template was quad-shaped all along; module-closed constant folding means the Turtle artifact still carries no graph slot. TRIG11 = imports turtle11 + nquads11 (diamond free): adds wrappedGraph ('{' triplesBlock '}' with inherited g bound to the label) + GRAPH keyword; every Turtle template streams into TriG unmodified because all were parametric in g. Where the W3C TriG REC rejects bare 4-column lines, the trig-w3c PROFILE excludes the nq:statement alternative — the N-Quads⊂TriG edge stays in the module algebra; the REC discrepancy is one profile triple, not a fork. N3 = imports turtle11 + n3-terms (Var, Formula(scope) ⊑ GraphT): formulae are two inherited-field updates (scope = fresh Skolem, g = formula(scope)); QUICK_VAR; paths desugar to fresh-object chaining productions (streamable by construction, addressing Q2 pressure); '=>' sugars log:implies over formula terms. RDF 1.2 AS A DELTA LAYER: rdf12-terms (tt constructor, dirLangString, LANG_DIR extendsTerminal LANGTAG) shared by ALL FOUR line/sugar syntaxes; ntriples12 = ntriples11 + rdf12-terms + (tripleTerm production, object-tt alternative, LANG_DIR); turtle12 = turtle11 + ntriples12 + (reifiedTriple, reifier, annotationChain with inherited pend, version directive as one env equation); nquads12/trig12 likewise — dirLang/tt well-formedness defined once, inherited by all. This resolves NOTE 3 completely: turtle11 is a real module, not comments — the '// rdf12' markers become the module boundary, and profiles handle the remaining carvings (canonical-ntriples, trig-w3c). COHERENCE THEOREMS (compiler-checked, both directions of every edge): profile(turtle12 − rdf12Δ) ≡ turtle11; profile(turtle11, fallbacks-only) ≡ ntriples11 (this plus a language-equivalence test against the W3C N-Triples EBNF discharges the pre-factoring fidelity risk); profile(trig12, no-braces) ≡ nquads12. TESTS ACCRETE UP: turtle11-tests imports ntriples11-tests — every (doc, expected) pair runs verbatim against the Turtle parser because extension is conservative (checked: no templates added to alternatives reachable in the imported language; violations need an explicit shu:nonConservative flag); crossing a triples→quads edge declares shu:resultLift shu:IntoDefaultGraph (trig12-tests imports turtle12-tests: actions unchanged, .nt results reinterpreted as .nq in the default graph — the lattice pays for its own test infrastructure, since N-Triples⊂N-Quads applies to the result-file format itself). NEGATIVES: shu:negativeScope shu:ThisLanguage (default) accretes DOWN profile edges only; each is mechanically re-decided by LL-table membership in supersets and re-tagged shu:negativeFor (trailing-dot-in-PN_LOCAL flips at Turtle, unclosed-IRIREF doesn't — all mechanical); shu:Hereditary negatives ("x"@en^^dt) are re-VERIFIED against every composed superset, catching a delta that accidentally legalizes them. NAME RESOLUTION: all names are IRIs; no scoping, shadowing, or import renaming; file-local prefixes are cosmetic; dangling IRI = compile error naming the missing module; "the definition" of a resource = the triples about it in the pinned closure, with named-graph provenance on every conflict report. SERIALIZER ACCRETION: print rules ride on alternatives, so importing the grammar imports the printer; sugar modules add guarded PrintRules; policies are modules (pretty-policy refines canonical-policy); restriction can only lose branches, so a profile's printer is total and only faster. NET RESULT: every syntax in the family is import + small delta; nothing defined twice; the W3C-suite anchor for N-Triples is paid once and inherited by every superset; a W3C WD change lands as an edit to exactly one module and propagates by import.


## Self-hosting & reflective closure

Two loops, one grounded bootstrap, all CI-checked. LOOP 1 — grammars are RDF, and Turtle is in the lattice: a v0.2 grammar IS a shu: graph normally written in Turtle, so "parse a grammar" = run the parser generated from mod:turtle12 (itself one of these graphs) and SHACL-validate against mod:shu-shapes. The v0.1 grammar/shuttle.ebnf and the hand-written meta front-end (packages/gen-js/src/meta.js) are retired as normative objects: THE META-SYNTAX IS TURTLE. Grammar well-formedness = SHACL conformance (arities, one-equation-per-occurrence, acyclicity, L-attributedness-as-a-shape, positional term typing incl. RDF 1.2, Skolem linearity, IRI-on-extensible-resources) — checkable by any RDF stack with zero Shuttle tooling; the deeper theorems (LL-disjointness, strictness, L3) sit above in the compiler. LOOP 2 — the .shu authoring surface is an ordinary module: mod:shu-surface is a Shuttle grammar whose EMITTED TRIPLES are the shu: grammar graph (text→RDF like any other syntax), and whose derived printer is the canonical graph→.shu pretty-printer — never hand-synchronized; any surface feature must have an RDF projection by construction, so the surface cannot drift. Its self-test: parse_shu(shu-surface.shu) ≅ ⟦mod:shu-surface⟧. BOOTSTRAP ORDER (finite, explicit trusted base): STAGE 0 — a tiny hand-written N-Triples reader plus the v0.1 reference IR interpreter (already the v0.1 trust anchor) load the distribution's module graphs shipped in canonical N-Triples (the most restricted lattice point is the bootstrap format — itself a lattice statement), specifically ntriples12.nt and turtle12.nt. ## The four v0.1 meta-language gaps, resolved

GAP 1 — skip-token designation (NOTE 1): `shu:skip true` on shu:Terminal resources. Skip status is ordinary module data: declared once on WS/COMMENT in mod:ntriples11, unioned up the whole lattice by import; per-terminal it is a shu:FunctionalGrammarProperty (a module cannot flip an imported terminal's skip status — conflict with both graph names); the lexical-conservativity check guarantees added terminals/skips never re-tokenize imported input. The v0.1 header-clause proposal (`skip WS, COMMENT ;`) survives as .shu surface sugar projecting to exactly these triples. GAP 2 — refined semTypes (NOTE 2): the special `term!` marker is dissolved. The term algebra's subtype lattice (SubjT, Iri, GraphT, TripleTermT ⊑ Term) is first-class RDF in mod:rdf-terms; any production's shu:syn attribute may carry ANY subtype, and "value + emissions" is simply a production that has both a typed syn attribute and attached templates — orthogonal, so `subjT!`, `graphT!` etc. exist automatically. SHACL positional typing (shu:s ⊑ SubjT, shu:p ⊑ Iri, shu:o ⊑ Term, shu:g ⊑ GraphT) then rejects misuse at the grammar graph itself, before compilation — strictly stronger than v0.1's downstream emit-argument check, and it is what makes RDF 1.2 well-formedness (tt in object position only, no literal predicates) a static data-level property stable under import. GAP 3 — per-alternative profile gating (NOTE 3): subsumed twice over. First, the primary mechanism is the lattice itself: the `// rdf12`-commented alternatives are not gated — they live in a different MODULE (turtle12's delta graph), so "turtle11 carved out of turtle12" is just... mod:turtle11, an actual graph, mechanically. Second, for carvings that are not lattice edges (canonical N-Triples, trig-w3c), shu:Profile provides declared restriction: shu:excludes/includesOnly over alternative/terminal IRIs, checked closed (no dangling references), sugar-only (guard-free fallbacks never excludable, so L3 and L ⊆ L(base) hold by construction), with the coherence theorem profile(turtle12 − rdf12Δ) ≡ turtle11 compiler-checked so the top-down and bottom-up views of every edge provably agree. GAP 4 — print-guard vocabulary (NOTE 4/Q4): the loose v0.1 guards become a CLOSED, IRI-identified guard vocabulary in mod:print-core — shu:listShaped, shu:freshSingleUse, shu:reifiesQuadPresent, shu:needsLongQuoting, shu:abbreviable — each guard resource carrying shu:requiresIndex triples naming the residual/window index that decides it (spec §7's derived indexes), so decidability is declared and machine-audited, and the generator computes exactly the indexes the closure's guards demand. shu:printGuard values must be these IRIs or shux:And/Or/Not compositions; extensions may mint new guard IRIs with declared index requirements (monotone, summary-graph-visible); the Q4 policy side (ordering, grouping, prefix mining, directive placement, layout) is the fixed shu:PrintPolicy property vocabulary on policy modules.
